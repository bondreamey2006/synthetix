
A company leave policy is a set of guidelines that explains how employees can take time off from work while ensuring that business operations continue smoothly. It includes different types of leave such as casual leave, sick leave, earned leave, and maternity or paternity leave. The policy also describes the procedure for applying for leave, which usually involves informing the manager in advance or submitting a request through the company’s HR system. It ensures fairness and transparency so that all employees understand their rights and responsibilities regarding leave.

For example, our company offers 20 days of paid leave per year to all full-time employees. This may include 12 days of casual or earned leave and 8 days of sick leave. Employees can use these leaves for personal needs, health issues, or emergencies, but they are expected to inform their supervisor and get approval before taking leave whenever possible.
